Right Neighborhood, Wrong Street

I wanted a career where I could work with people, use my natural empathy, and be a creative problem solver. No, I didn’t jump right into the field of UX design. I got my Master’s degree in Communication Sciences and became a medical Speech Pathologist.

Three years later, I was using those important skills and I was good at my job. So why was I feeling like something was missing? It turns out Speech Therapy put me in the right neighborhood, but I was on the wrong street.

I'm grateful for the Speech Therapy field, not just for the transferable skills I learned, but because it led me directly to UX design. We were required to document in an Electronic Medical Record system that was the definition of poor user experience. I eventually volunteered to work with the web development team to redesign the entire documentation system. The rest was history.

An image taken at an in-service I facilitated as a clinician.

I enrolled in an immersive UX design apprenticeship to acquire those important design-specific skills. As mentioned earlier, I still pull from my experiences and skills as a Speech-Pathologist. But now I am able to marry technical skills, design aesthetics, and empathy to create real solutions for people.

To me, UX design is more than just creating products. It is about truly understanding people and their needs to make impactful solutions that really count. The future is digital and I am excited to have a hand in shaping that future.
